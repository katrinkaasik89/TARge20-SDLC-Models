Kanban: Advantages and Disadvantages

Advantages Disadvantages
⚡ Better visibility. Visualization is an important Kanban practice, and the most recognizable feature of the method is the Kanban board. ⛔ It’s more of a technique that is rather loosely structured and so may not be the best option for new teams that typically need a more prescriptive approach.
⚡ ‘In Progress’ section may be expanded and have ‘analysis’, ‘coding’, ‘testing’, ‘for approval’, and ‘ready for release’ columns. ⛔ Once the board gets messy or overcomplicated, using Kanban may become a very negative and discouraging experience.
⚡ The Kanban technique fights any temptations to cut corners and skip some important steps in the process. ⛔ The columns on the board represent stages, but you can’t really tell how long each phase is going to last, because there is no defined time frame.
